Every distortion circuit reshapes the waveform. The shape of that reshaping determines which harmonics are created. Today you will connect three views of the same phenomenon: the transfer function (input → output mapping), the waveform (time domain), and the spectrum (frequency domain).

Labs: localhost:3000/fuzzbox-lab.html  •  localhost:3000/fuzzbox-transfer.html
Reference
Symmetric vs. Asymmetric
Symmetry What It Means Harmonics Created Examples
Symmetric Positive and negative halves are shaped identically Odd only (3rd, 5th, 7th…) Hard clip, soft clip, foldback
Asymmetric Positive and negative halves are shaped differently Even + odd (2nd, 3rd, 4th…) Tube bias, half-wave rectify
