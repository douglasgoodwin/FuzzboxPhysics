# How to talk about distortion

## FRAMEWORK: Extremes, Strain, and Saturation -- From Voice to Instruments to Circuits

---

## PART 1: Core Principles -- What's Actually Happening

### The Physics of Extremity

Every system you've discussed involves **pushing a physical medium beyond its linear operating range**:

| System | Medium | Normal Operation | Extreme Operation | Result |
|--------|--------|-----------------|-------------------|--------|
| **Vocal cords** | Biological tissue | Periodic vibration | Aperiodic/chaotic vibration | Roughness, breaks, distortion |
| **Lungs/breath** | Air pressure | Steady flow | Interrupted/excessive flow | Gasping, running out, hyperventilation |
| **Guitar amp** | Magnetic/electronic | Linear amplification | Clipping/saturation | Harmonic distortion, compression |
| **Tape** | Magnetic particles | Linear recording | Magnetic saturation | Compression, warmth, distortion |
| **Circuit** | Electronic components | Within design specs | Pushed to limits | Instability, oscillation, failure modes |

**The unifying concept**: **NONLINEARITY MADE AUDIBLE**

When systems operate normally, output is proportional to input (linear). When pushed to extremes, output becomes **disproportionate** -- small changes create large effects, harmonics appear, instability emerges.

**This is where the interesting stuff happens.**

---

### Four Categories of Extremes

#### 1. SATURATION (The System Can't Get Louder)

- **Voice**: Son House pushing volume until voice distorts
- **Instrument**: Guitar amp turned to 10, signal clipping
- **Circuit**: Amplifier rails hitting power supply limits
- **What's happening**: The medium is asked for more output than it can physically produce
- **Sonic result**: Harmonic distortion, compression, "warmth"

#### 2. DEPLETION (The System Runs Out of Resources)

- **Voice**: Bessie Smith's breath running out at phrase ends
- **Instrument**: Drum hit so hard the head can't return to position fast enough (pitch drops)
- **Circuit**: Capacitor draining faster than it can recharge
- **What's happening**: Energy source exhausted before signal ends
- **Sonic result**: Decay, pitch/timbre shift, collapse

#### 3. INTERFERENCE (The System Fights Itself)

- **Voice**: Patti Smith hitting chest while singing
- **Instrument**: Feedback (output feeding back into input)
- **Circuit**: Oscillation from positive feedback
- **What's happening**: Output affects input, creating unstable loop
- **Sonic result**: Feedback tones, instability, chaos

#### 4. MECHANICAL FAILURE (The System Can't Track Fast Enough)

- **Voice**: Nina Hagen's rapid register shifts (muscles can't keep up)
- **Instrument**: Bowing violin so fast the string can't settle into periodic vibration (scratch tone)
- **Circuit**: Signal changing faster than components can respond (slew rate limiting)
- **What's happening**: Rate of change exceeds system's response time
- **Sonic result**: Glitching, roughness, "digital" artifacts from analog systems
