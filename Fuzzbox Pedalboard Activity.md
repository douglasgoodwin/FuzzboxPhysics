# Fuzzbox Pedalboard Activity

## Goal

Design an effects **chain** (a sequence of stages) on the fuzzbox pedalboard that gives a specific sonic “personality,” using your understanding of reverb, echo, “vocal strain,” and transfer functions.[1][2]

***

## Key ideas

- A stage is a processing block that changes your signal in some way (tone, dynamics, time, or space).[1]
- A pedalboard is a chain of stages; order matters because each stage acts on the result of the previous one.[3]
- Think at three levels: sound sample → transfer function (what curve/behavior?) → stage in a chain (where does it sit and why?).[1]

***

## Warm‑up listening (whole class)

1. Listen to a dry clip, then to a simple chain on the fuzzbox pedalboard.  
2. Describe in words:  
   - What changed in tone (bright/dull, smooth/strained)?  
   - What changed in space (close/far, small room/huge hall)?  
   - Which stage do you think is doing most of the work?  

***

## Design briefs (choose one)

Your group picks one and builds a 3–4 stage chain that fits it.

1. “Intimate, broken voice in a huge space”  
2. “Distant, calm voice in a tight metallic room”  
3. “Harsh, up‑front shout that smears into the distance”  
4. “Soft, warm voice that suddenly cracks on peaks”  

***

## Your task (in groups)

On the fuzzbox pedalboard:

- Choose and order 3–4 stages.  
- Tweak parameters until the sound matches your brief as closely as possible.  
- Pay attention to what happens when you move a stage earlier vs. later in the chain (e.g., strain before reverb vs. after).[2][3]

***

## What to document

For your final chain, write:

1. Stage order (left → right), naming each stage.  
2. One sentence per stage:  
   - “This stage does X to the sound” (e.g., adds upper harmonics, repeats, room tail).  
3. A transfer‑function intuition:  
   - Sketch or briefly describe the curve/behavior you imagine for one “vocal strain” or distortion‑like stage.  
4. A short reflection (3–4 sentences):  
   - Why this order?  
   - What changed when you tried a different order?  
   - How does the whole chain behave as a single instrument or voice?  

***

## Share‑out

Each group will:

- Play 5–10 seconds of dry → processed audio.  
- State your brief and show your stage order.  
- Explain, in one or two sentences, which stage is most critical for the personality you created.
