# Fuzzbox Physics
## Transducers and Alternative Ways to Record Sound

Today: how different sensors define “signal,” erase other events, and change what a recording can be *about*.

---

# Thesis
## A microphone is a decision system

A transducer does not “capture the world.”
It converts one slice of physics into voltage.

Questions we will keep asking:
- What counts as signal?
- What becomes noise, silence, or irrelevant?
- What is missing compared to an air microphone?

---

# Three terms
## Keep these separate

- **Medium**: what carries vibration/variation (air, water, solid, EM field, radio spectrum)
- **Sensor / transducer**: device converting that variation into voltage
- **Capture chain**: preamp/interface/encoding + filtering that further “decides”

---

# Baseline case
## Air microphones

Air-coupled pressure variations.

Typical cues:
- distance and perspective
- reverberation and room size
- foreground / midground / background scene

Use this as the control for every comparison.

---

# Alternative 1
## Solid-coupled recording (contact mics)

Couples vibration through solids rather than air.

Often foregrounds:
- resonance of metal/wood/glass/structure
- creaks, drags, long decays
- internal “mechanical detail”

Often suppresses:
- voices, wind, traffic, room air

Key idea:
- **boundary as instrument**
- surfaces become de facto “speakers”

---

# Contact mic reality check
## What your ear doesn’t tell you

A piezo disk on a surface can be nearly deaf to the room,
but intensely sensitive to structure-borne vibration.

Small changes in coupling:
- clamping pressure
- where it touches
- material thickness
can completely change the “mix.”

---

# Alternative 2
## Hydrophones (underwater recording)

Water-coupled pressure variations.

Often foregrounds:
- low-frequency pressure events
- proximity to the capsule
- dense, “thick” sound fields

Often suppresses:
- above-surface “air” cues
- familiar room-distance perspective

Key idea:
- **medium as filter**
- water rewrites spectral balance and perspective

---

# Alternative 3
## Electromagnetic induction (EM listening)

Not sound pressure.

You are “miking”:
- power supplies
- switching regulators
- motors and screens
- security gates and infrastructure

Key idea:
- **infrastructure as environment**
- the “room” is circuitry and the grid

---

# Alternative 4
## Radio interception

Not a room. A communications channel.

You are hearing:
- tuning, bandwidth, modulation
- interference and noise floors
- fragments of speech not “addressed” to you

Key idea:
- physics + protocol determine what gets through

---

# The hinge concept
## Reversibility

Some transducers are symmetric electromechanical devices.

Speaker as microphone:
- mechanical motion -> electrical signal

Microphone as speaker (sometimes):
- electrical signal -> mechanical motion

This is not just a trick.
It reveals what a transducer *is*.

# Demonstration idea 1
## “Backwards speaker” as mic

Connect a raw loudspeaker to an amp input.

Try:
- claps near the cone
- voice at different distances
- tapping the frame

Listen for:
- what gets emphasized
- what disappears
- how “perspective” changes

---

# Demonstration idea 2
## Contact mic: boundary as instrument

Put a contact mic on:
- a window
- a table leg
- a pipe
- a metal railing

Do:
- light fingertip taps vs scraping
- changing pressure / position

Question:
- what did the surface decide to amplify?

# Demonstration idea 3
## Induction pickup: infrastructure as place

Move an induction coil near:
- laptop power brick
- monitor
- fluorescent ballast
- phone charging cable

Question:
- what “landmarks” become audible?
